Credit Card Analytics Dashboard – Power BI Project
🚀 Project Overview

This project involves building two interactive Power BI dashboards using Customer and Credit Card Transaction datasets.

The objective was to create meaningful, actionable insights about:

Customer demographics and credit behavior

Credit card spending patterns

Revenue and transaction performance

Both dashboards are fully interactive and allow filtering by Gender, Age Group, Income Group, Week, and Spending Category.

The focus was on clean visual design, strong DAX calculations, and business-driven insights.

📊 Dashboard 1 – Credit Card Customer Report
🎯 Objective

To analyze customer demographics, income distribution, credit behavior, and ownership patterns while enabling filtering by gender.

📌 Key Features

KPI Cards:

Total Customers (10.29K)

Average Customer Satisfaction Score (3.19)

Income distribution by Customer Job

Customer count by Marital Status

Education Level distribution

Dependent count distribution

Geographic distribution (Map)

Car Owner vs House Owner comparison

Filters: Gender, Age Group, Income Group

💳 Dashboard 2 – Credit Card Transaction Report
🎯 Objective

To analyze transaction behavior, spending trends, card performance, and revenue generation patterns.

📌 Key Features

KPI Cards:

Total Transaction Amount (46M)

Total Revenue (9.99M)

Customer Acquisition Cost (991K)

Average Utilization Ratio (0.27)

Revenue by Card Category (Blue, Silver, Gold, Platinum)

Interest Earned by Quarter

Transaction Amount by State (Map)

Delinquent Account Distribution

Credit Limit vs Transaction Amount by Age Group

Filters: Gender, Week Number, Expense Type

⚠️ Challenges Faced

🧹 Data Cleaning – Handling missing values and ensuring customer and transaction datasets were properly linked via Customer ID.

🔗 Data Modeling – Creating accurate relationships between demographic and transaction tables.

📐 DAX Calculations – Designing calculated columns for Age Group, Income Group, and weekly revenue comparisons.

🎨 Dashboard Design – Maintaining clarity and readability while including multiple slicers and visuals.

🔍 Key Insights
📊 Customer Dashboard Insights

Business professionals generate the highest income contribution among job categories.

Married customers form the largest customer segment.

Customers in their 40s and 50s have higher credit limits and transaction amounts compared to younger groups.

Delinquency rate is relatively low, indicating stable credit behavior overall.

💳 Transaction Dashboard Insights

Blue card category generates the highest revenue compared to other categories.

Transaction activity is stronger in certain states, indicating geographic concentration.

Interest earned shows a declining trend across quarters.

Middle-aged customers (40s–50s) contribute the highest transaction volume.

💡 Recommendations

🎯 Target High-Value Age Groups – Focus marketing campaigns on customers in their 40s and 50s who generate higher transaction volumes.

💳 Upsell Premium Cards – Encourage Blue card customers to upgrade to Gold or Platinum for higher revenue potential.

📍 Geographic Expansion – Target high-performing states with customized offers and loyalty programs.

📉 Monitor Interest Decline – Investigate reasons for decreasing interest earnings and optimize credit strategies.

👥 Segment by Income & Gender – Develop personalized offers based on income groups and spending behavior.
